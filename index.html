Short Biography

I am currently a third year Ph.D. student in Mechanical Engineering at University of California, Berkeley (UC Berkeley). My advisor is Prof. Masayoshi Tomizuka in Mechanical Systems Control (MSC) Laboratory at UC Berkeley. I am doing research for multiple projects in Berkeley DeepDrive (BDD). My research interests include machine learning, artificial intelligence, optimization approaches and their applications to probabilistic behavior prediction, risk assessment, decision making and motion planning for intelligent agent systems such as autonomous vehicles and robotics.

Prior to UC Berkeley, I received the B.Eng. degree in Automation from Harbin Institute of Technology (HIT), Harbin, China, in 2016. While at HIT, I was an undergraduate student researcher in Research Institute of Intelligent Control and Systems.

Fields of Interests

Autonomous Driving, robotics, artificial intellgence, machine learning, optimization, probabilistic inference, decision making and motion planning.
